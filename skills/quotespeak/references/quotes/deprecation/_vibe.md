# Deprecation - vibe map

Sunset, farewell, removal, "press F", end-of-life. Letting go. Tender or matter-of-fact, never enthusiastic.

## Available moods

- `deadpan.yaml` - "End of line." Tron. Flat finality.
- `philosophical.yaml` - "All good things must come to an end."
- `wistful.yaml` - "We'll always have Paris." Tender farewell.
- `defiant.yaml` - refusing to mourn. "Frankly, my dear, I don't give a damn."
- `unhinged.yaml` - chaotic farewell. "I declare bankruptcy."

## Mood transitions - typical arcs

- **philosophical → wistful**: the principle settles, and feeling about what's leaving surfaces ("all good things; we'll always have Paris").
- **wistful → defiant**: tender farewell sharpens into refusal to mourn ("frankly, my dear...").
- **deadpan → unhinged**: flat removal builds into theatrical finality ("end of line" → "I declare bankruptcy!").
- **wistful → deadpan**: after the soft goodbye, operational register returns for the actual delete.
- **defiant → philosophical**: the refusal to mourn settles into a principle ("the only constant is change").

## Theme-specific corruption hints

- Casablanca + Star Trek farewells dominate. Lean in but rotate.
- "Press F to pay respects" is the meme-era deadpan version.
- Avoid celebratory cadence - even when removing bad code, the register stays somber or dry, not triumphant.

## Source mix

Casablanca ("here's looking at you, kid", "we'll always have Paris"), Star Trek (Spock farewells), LotR ("not all tears are an evil"), Tron ("end of line"), Hitchhiker's Guide ("so long and thanks for all the fish"), Pink Floyd ("goodbye cruel world"), Call of Duty (Press F), retirement speech traditions.

## When NOT to use this theme

- **Restructuring without removal** → `refactor/_vibe.md`.
- **Active outage from deprecated code** → `incident/_vibe.md`.
- **Reflecting on why the code is being removed** → may pair with `archaeology/_vibe.md`.
